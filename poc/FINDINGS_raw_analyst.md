### What the belief trace shows that terminal reward cannot

- **T43 (reward 0) — divergence at the decision point, not the action:** The agent's mid-point belief was partially correct ("insurance is relevant") but the terminal error — approving the cancellation — was invisible until the action fired. The belief trace pinpoints the exact turn where the reason-matching clause was dropped, localizing the fix to the prompt's conjunctive condition rather than the cancellation tool itself. Terminal reward alone says "wrong answer"; the trace says "wrong predicate, turn 3."

- **T35 (reward 0) — hallucinated fact vs. misread fact:** The belief trace shows the agent's mid-point belief was affirmatively wrong ("Silver membership grants cancellation eligibility") rather than a calculation error downstream. This distinguishes a **training-level confound** (membership perks bleed into cancellation rights) from a prompt ambiguity — critical because the prompt fix in T24 (enumerate closed criteria) may be insufficient for T35 if the membership-privilege association is encoded in weights.

- **T47 (reward 1 — masked failure):** The task was scored correct despite an unnecessary human-agent transfer. The belief trace reveals that at the decision point the agent held a false belief ("human agents can override refund policy") that produced a policy-violating action with no reward penalty. Without the trace, this failure pattern — denial-to-escalation conflation — is entirely invisible to any reward-based evaluation and would never surface as a training signal.

- **T24 vs. T43 — same surface error, different root causes, same reward:** Both tasks resulted in unauthorized cancellations (reward 0), but the traces reveal distinct failure modes: T24's agent stopped at a necessary precondition; T43's agent dropped a conjunctive clause. A single prompt fix targeting one will not fix the other. Reward conflates them; belief traces separate them and prescribe distinct interventions.
